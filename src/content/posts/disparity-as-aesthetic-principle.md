---
title: "Disparity as Aesthetic Principle: Zizek, the Abject, and the Repelling X"
date: 2026-03-02
tags: [philosophy, aesthetics]
---
Slavoj Zizek's concept of disparity, developed through a Hegelian diagnostic of contemporary culture, identifies three domains in which fundamental ontological difference persists and disrupts the homogenizing logic of late capitalism: the ontological, the aesthetic, and the theological-political.

In the aesthetic domain specifically, Zizek locates disparity in what he calls *the repelling X.* That element which resists full integration into the work's surface coherence and which is variously named as the ugly, the disgusting, the abject.

## The Condition of Aesthetic Tension

This is not incidental. The repelling X is not a failure of aesthetic judgment but its very condition, the element that generates aesthetic tension precisely by refusing to be domesticated into formal resolution.

> To be real is to be contradictory, to be a member of a set that does not include you.

Timothy Morton extends this logic ecologically. Reality itself is structured by disparity, and the artwork that honestly engages with reality must therefore incorporate its own dissonance.

## Activating Contradiction

This suggests a practice oriented not toward the production of harmonious objects but toward the activation of internal contradictions. Works whose materials, processes, and histories pull against one another. Where the collaged fragment resists the surface it is pressed into. Where digital and analog are in genuine tension rather than seamless synthesis.

The abject element in the work is not something to be resolved but something to be preserved, because it is precisely the locus of the work's contact with the Real.
